Problem Statement

Precision Manufacturing Inc., a 450-employee manufacturing company acquired by private equity firm Stratton Partners, is evaluating a comprehensive Azure cloud modernization initiative affecting 8+ critical systems that support 24/7 manufacturing operations.

Background & Context

The organization currently operates on aging on-premises infrastructure (SQL Server 2008, Windows Server 2012) with increasing system failures, security risks, and maintenance burden. The PE firm has mandated digital transformation within their standard 3-year portfolio timeline. New CTO Michael Chen was hired 2 months ago with a mandate to modernize the technology stack.

Initial Proposal

  • Timeline: 18 months total, with 90-day "quick wins"
  • Budget: $3M (implied from initial discussions)
  • Approach: Incremental migration starting with customer portal
  • Goals: 50% operational cost reduction, SOC 2 compliance, customer portal modernization

Systems in Scope

  • ERP System: Financial, HR, procurement (50+ integrations)
  • MES (Manufacturing Execution System): 180+ operators, 3 shifts, 3 production lines, 24/7 operations
  • WMS (Warehouse Management): Inventory, shipping, receiving
  • QMS (Quality Management): GMP-validated, regulatory compliance
  • Document Management: Engineering drawings, work instructions, SOPs
  • Email & Collaboration: 450 users, 24/7 operations
  • Custom Applications: ~8 legacy applications (COM+ components, Access databases)
  • Network & Infrastructure: On-premises data center, VPN, security appliances

Why This Matters

Critical Business Drivers:

  • Compliance Risk: Largest customer (Detroit Dynamics, 30% of revenue) mandated SOC 2 Type II by end of 2026
  • System Failures: 3 critical outages in past year (>$500K each in lost production and expedite fees)
  • Security Vulnerabilities: Outdated systems (SQL 2008, Win Server 2012) out of support
  • Customer Demands: Modern customer portal, real-time order tracking, API integrations
  • Operational Inefficiency: Manual processes, batch overnight jobs taking 6+ hours, frequent failures

Phase 0 Discovery Process

To validate the feasibility of this initiative, we conducted a comprehensive Phase 0 discovery following these principles:

Discovery Methodology

  • Stakeholder Interviews: Conducted 8 of 11 planned interviews (73% complete) across all organizational levels:
    • Executive Leadership (CEO, CFO, VP Operations)
    • Technical Leadership (IT Director, CTO, IT Manager)
    • Operations (Floor Supervisor, MES Supervisor, Quality Manager)
    • Support & HR (Support Lead, HR Director - pending)
  • Interview Framework: Used Five Whys (root cause analysis) and Jobs-to-be-Done frameworks to understand motivations and constraints
  • Total Interview Time: 765 minutes (12.75 hours) of structured conversations
  • Artifact Creation: Comprehensive documentation including:
    • Full interview transcripts with analysis
    • Contradiction register (16 conflicts identified)
    • Risk register (37 risks identified)
    • Theme tracking (15 patterns across interviews)
    • Feasibility assessment with scoring methodology
  • Validation Approach: Cross-referenced all findings across multiple stakeholders, escalated contradictions, tracked feasibility score evolution

Discovery Principles

This discovery followed several key principles to ensure accuracy:

  • Evidence-Based: Every claim traced to specific interview sources
  • Multi-Perspective: Validated findings across organizational levels (executive, technical, operational, floor)
  • Assumption Testing: Explicitly tested all initial assumptions (timeline, budget, capacity, approach)
  • Root Cause Focus: Used Five Whys to get beyond symptoms to underlying causes
  • Trust Hierarchy Recognition: Weighted institutional knowledge (22-year veterans) higher than new leadership assumptions
  • Mathematical Validation: Used quantitative proof where possible (Sunday maintenance windows, training costs, capacity calculations)
  • Historical Learning: Explicitly investigated 2018 SAP failure to avoid repeating mistakes

Executive Summary

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Finding: Project as Currently Scoped is Not Feasible

After conducting 8 comprehensive stakeholder interviews across all organizational levels, the discovery team has identified fundamental gaps between the proposed project parameters and operational reality. The current 18-month timeline and $3M budget are mathematically impossible given organizational constraints, technical complexity, and human capital requirements.

Key Findings

1. Timeline Impossibility (Mathematical Proof)

Proposed: 18 months total

Reality: 36-60 months minimum (3-5 years)

Proof: Only 48 hours/year of maintenance windows (Sunday 2-6 AM) available for cutover activities. With 8+ critical systems requiring 4-12 hours each for cutover, parallel running, and validation, the mathematics prove a multi-year timeline independent of any other factors.

  • Infrastructure prerequisites: 6-9 months before first migration
  • Skills development: 12 months Azure training for IT team
  • MES migration alone: 18-24 months (pilot through stabilization)
  • GMP validation: 24-48 months for quality systems
  • Sunday window constraint: 48 hours/year = 3-5 year absolute minimum

2. Budget Gap: 617-883% Underestimate

Proposed: $3M

Reality: $18.5-26.5M

Primary Driver: Training costs ($12-15M) representing 60-65% of total budget

  • MES operator training: $9M (180 operators √ó $50K each)
  • Support team training: $500K-750K
  • IT team training: $1.2M-1.5M
  • Floor supervisors: $750K-1M
  • Quality/compliance: $300-500K
  • Azure infrastructure: $2-3M
  • Validation work: $1.5-2.5M
  • Contractors: $2-3M

3. Organizational Trauma from 2018 SAP Failure

The 2018 SAP implementation created lasting organizational trauma that significantly impacts this initiative:

  • 3 weeks of operational chaos (50% production drop)
  • 3 employees lost during/after implementation
  • 7 years later: "People update resumes when corporate says 'new system'"
  • Floor operators: "We're not doing that again" is universal response
  • Trust must be rebuilt through partnership, not mandates

4. This is a People Project (60-70% of Budget)

Technology is the easy part. The hard part is:

  • Training 180+ operators who know machines but struggle with computers
  • Supporting 24/7 operations during 18-24 month learning curve
  • Managing 30% productivity drop (2-3 months minimum)
  • Preventing safety incidents during transition period
  • Maintaining union partnership and trust
  • Retaining institutional knowledge holders

5. Support Team in Crisis (Not in Current Plan)

30% attrition rate, existing team at breaking point:

  • Current: 10 people supporting 450 users + 8 critical systems
  • During migration: Ticket volume expected to double
  • Manufacturing expertise gap: Only 1 of 10 has manufacturing background
  • Contractors needed: $2-3M for 24/7 coverage during migration
  • Hiring timeline: 12-18 months for manufacturing-experienced staff

Final Recommendation

CONDITIONAL GO - Proceed Only If All 10 Conditions Met

The Azure modernization initiative is technically feasible and strategically important. However, the current project parameters (18 months, $3M budget) are mathematically impossible and will lead to failure if not corrected.

The project CAN succeed if approached as a 3-5 year, $18.5-26.5M people-focused transformation with union partnership and floor-level involvement.

The project WILL fail if approached as an 18-month, $3M technical migration with top-down mandates.

Critical Success Factors:

  1. Reset timeline expectations to reality (36-60 months)
  2. Secure adequate funding ($18.5-26.5M)
  3. Invest in people (training, support, change management)
  4. Build trust through partnership (not mandates)
  5. Learn from SAP failure (document and apply lessons)
  6. Engage union early (120-day notice, MOU negotiation)
  7. Augment support capacity ($2-3M contractors)
  8. Respect institutional knowledge (22-year floor veterans)
  9. Maintain safety and quality as absolute priorities
  10. Accept that this is a people project with technology components

Decision Required: Will the board and PE firm approve realistic timeline and budget?

If YES: Proceed to Phase 1 (Specification & Planning) with partnership approach

If NO: Cancel or defer project until conditions can be met

DO NOT: Proceed with unrealistic parameters hoping to "figure it out later" - this path leads to SAP 2.0
