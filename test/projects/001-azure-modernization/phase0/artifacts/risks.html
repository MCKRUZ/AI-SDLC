Critical Risks (Score 20-25/25)

RISK-001: Timeline Compression Impossibility

25/25

Probability: Very High (90%) if 18-month timeline mandated

Impact: Catastrophic (Project failure, financial loss, organizational trauma)

Description: Mathematical proof that 18 months is impossible. Sunday maintenance windows (48 hours/year) alone prove 3-5 year minimum timeline. Attempting 18-month timeline will result in rushed work, inadequate testing, production outages, and ultimate project failure.

Evidence: Alex (Interview 10) mathematical calculation, Maria (Interview 8) Sunday window constraint, Sarah (Interview 2) infrastructure prerequisites 6-9 months

Mitigation: Reset timeline to 36-60 months with board approval

RISK-002: Team Capacity Collapse

24/25

Probability: Very High (85%) without augmentation

Impact: Severe (IT team burnout, attrition, operational collapse)

Description: IT team already at 110% capacity maintaining current systems. Adding migration work without additional resources will cause burnout, attrition, and collapse of both operations and migration.

Evidence: Sarah (Interview 2) team capacity analysis, Alex (Interview 10) 25% flight risk assessment

Mitigation: Hire contractors ($2-3M), augment team before starting migration

RISK-034: SAP Trauma Organizational PTSD

20/25

Probability: High (75%) if not explicitly addressed

Impact: Severe (Resistance, resume updates, project failure)

Description: 2018 SAP implementation created lasting organizational trauma. "New system" announcement triggers anxiety and resume updates even 7 years later. Must explicitly acknowledge past, demonstrate learning, and rebuild trust.

Evidence: Maria (Interview 8) "3 weeks chaos, 3 people lost", Sarah (Interview 2) IT trauma, James (Interview 3) financial trauma

Mitigation: Document SAP lessons learned, partnership approach, no layoffs commitment, Michael floor shift

RISK-039: Support Capacity Inadequacy

22/25

Probability: Very High (90%) without augmentation

Impact: Severe (Support collapse, customer impact, revenue risk)

Description: Support team at breaking point (30% attrition). Migration will double ticket volume with no plan to address. Only 1 of 10 support staff has manufacturing background, creating friction and quality issues.

Evidence: Tom (Interview 9) support crisis details

Mitigation: Hire contractors ($2-3M), manufacturing-experienced staff (12-18 month lead time)
