Executive Summary

TechFlow Solutions' customer support portal, built in 2019 for 200 customers, is now serving 800+ customers and causing operational breakdown. Support response times average 3-4 days (violating 24-hour SLA), contributing to 8% monthly churn versus target of <5%.

Problem in One Sentence: TechFlow's outdated support portal cannot scale to serve growing customer base, causing support team inefficiency, customer frustration, and unsustainable churn that threatens company valuation and funding.

Root Cause Analysis (Five Whys)

Why #1: Why is this a problem?
Customers get blocked when they can't get timely support, lose productivity, get frustrated
Why #2: Why does that matter?
Being blocked repeatedly causes customers to evaluate competitors and eventually churn, losing revenue
Why #3: Why is that significant?
Support team can't respond faster - 8 agents for 800 customers with inefficient tools that waste 15-20 minutes per ticket
Why #4: Why does that create concern?
Support portal from 2019 wasn't built for scale - no AI, no intelligent search, manual processes, information scattered across 4 systems
Why #5: Why is this a priority now?
Company systematically under-invested in support infrastructure for 18 months. Now at crisis point: churn affecting valuation, board mandate to fix by Q3, Series C funding at risk

Success Criteria (Phase 1)

Metric Current Target Improvement

Proposed Solution (Phase 1 Scope)

  • AI-Powered Response Suggestions: GPT-4 integration to suggest responses based on ticket context and historical data
  • Intelligent Search: Azure Cognitive Search with semantic understanding to replace keyword-only matching
  • Unified Customer View: Single interface consolidating data from Zendesk, Salesforce, Product DB, and Documentation
  • Modern Tech Stack: React 18, .NET 8, real-time features, mobile optimization
  • Real-time Collaboration: Agent-to-agent communication and ticket handoff features

002-cto-interview.md Mike Rodriguez - CTO

Length: 501 lines | Date: January 16, 2025

üîç Key Insights
  • Technical Debt: Portal built as MVP in 2019 for 200 customers, now serving 800+ without infrastructure investment
  • Architecture Limitations: Monolithic design, no caching, every request hits database
  • Error Rate: 200-300 errors/day in Sentry logs requiring 120 hours/quarter firefighting
  • Technology Gap: No AI/ML capabilities, keyword-only search, no real-time features

Root Cause Identified: "Portal architecture was never designed for scale. Built as quick MVP with no intelligent tooling. Engineering resources went to new customer acquisition (features) rather than retention (support tooling) for 18 months despite Support Lead's documented requests."

003-support-lead-interview.md Jennifer Park - Head of Customer Support

Length: 545 lines | Date: January 17, 2025

üîç Key Insights
  • Workflow Inefficiency: Agents waste 15-20 minutes per ticket on manual tasks (should be 2-3 minutes)
  • Context Switching: 4-system workflow (Zendesk, Salesforce, Product DB, Documentation) wastes 2-3 hours/agent/day
  • Team Morale: Support team feels tools are working against them, contributing to burnout
  • Capacity Crisis: 60 tickets/day incoming, only 50/day capacity = growing backlog
  • Priority #1: Unified customer view to eliminate context switching (initially not in CTO's Phase 1 proposal)

Root Cause Identified: "Support team pain wasn't visible to leadership until it manifested as customer churn and revenue loss. My requests for better tools were documented but deprioritized for 18 months. Now we're in reactive crisis management."

004-support-agent-interview.md Alex Thompson - Support Agent

  • Frustration: Feels like fighting the tools to help customers rather than having tools help them
  • Hope: Excited about AI suggestions and unified view, believes it will dramatically improve efficiency

Root Cause Identified: "Tools force me to spend 15-20 minutes per ticket searching across 4 systems and re-typing answers I've given 50 times. I created personal workarounds just to survive, but I shouldn't have to. The portal should help me help customers."

Critical Risks

HIGH

Description: Team has no prior GPT-4 integration experience, creating risk of poor prompt engineering, unexpected costs, and quality issues.

HIGH

Description: Q3 2025 deadline allows 20-24 weeks, but recommended implementation is 12-14 weeks for Phase 1, leaving minimal buffer.

MEDIUM

Description: Five system integrations (Zendesk, Salesforce, Product DB, Azure AI Search, OpenAI) represent higher complexity than initially scoped.

Mitigation: Technical spike tasks in week 1, allocate 2 weeks per integration, have senior engineer lead integration work.

Risk #4: Production System Criticality
MEDIUM

Description: Portal serves 800 active customers in 24/5 support operation. Any downtime or defects directly impact customer satisfaction.

Mitigation: Robust rollback strategy, comprehensive testing, phased rollout, feature flags for gradual activation.
